To: Tenacious CEO & CFO Date: April 25, 2026 From: 10Academy Week 10 — Conversion Engine Build Re: Pilot Deployment Decision — AI-Driven Outbound Prospecting System

Executive Summary

The Tenacious Conversion Engine classifies prospects into four ICP segments using enrichment signals (layoffs, funding rounds, leadership changes, AI maturity scores), generates honesty-constrained cold emails, and routes warm replies via automated classification or human handoff. On the τ²-Bench retail held-out slice (n=20, sealed), the system achieved pass@1 = 0.4000 with 95% CI [0.22, 0.61] — a +0.1333 delta above the same-model dev baseline — and moved the critical SOC-01 honesty probe from FAIL to PASS after the Phase 5 Python-enforced post-generation scan. Recommendation: proceed to a 30-day Segment 2 pilot at 40 leads/week; do not expand to all four segments until the assertive-claim-regen-failed edge case (§11) is resolved.

§7 — Four Failure Modes τ²-Bench Does Not Capture

ModeWhat Happens in ProductionWhy τ²-Bench Misses ItBusiness Impact
1. Stale hiring signal Playwright scrapes job boards 2–8h before send. If the open role is filled in that window, the agent emails "I see you're hiring a Senior ML Engineer" about a closed req. Estimated 5–10% of signals are stale at send time. τ²-Bench tasks are synchronous and stateless — no asynchronous signal decay between scrape and action. Prospect disproves claim in 10 seconds. Immediate hard-no; credibility permanently lost.
2. Undisclosed in-house-only policy The ICP classifier disqualifies companies with explicit anti-offshore JD signals. Companies with implicit in-house-only policies pass through and receive a vendor pitch. Est. ~5% of Seg 4 targets. τ²-Bench simulates cooperative retail customers — no implicit cultural rejection patterns appear in its task set. Sharp opt-out and LinkedIn callout risk. Costs one outreach slot plus potential network-level brand damage.
3. Multi-contact conflicting pitches CTO + CFO at the same company are classified into different segments and receive different angles (Seg 3 leadership pitch vs. Seg 1 growth pitch). They compare notes in an internal Slack channel. MTL probes (MTL-01–03) verify data isolation but not cross-contact pitch consistency. τ²-Bench tests single-contact threads only. Both threads terminate. Tenacious blocked at domain level. Full ACV at risk.
4. Re-engagement with stale cache reengage_1 requires "one new data point from fresh enrichment." check_reengagement_eligible() does not validate cache freshness — the "new" fact may be the same one from the cold email sent 45 days prior. τ²-Bench does not model multi-week outbound sequence state or enrichment cache TTL. Tasks reset state on each run. Prospect recognizes the stale fact. Credibility loss + permanent hard-no. Silent failure — no error raised.

§8 — Public-Signal Lossiness (AI Maturity Scoring)

False Positive — Loud-but-Shallow

A company posts "Our AI-First Engineering Culture" blogs and 3 Data Engineer JDs using "LLM infrastructure." ai_maturity scorer assigns score = 2 (the Seg 4 qualifying threshold). In practice, the company is rebranding basic ETL. Agent sends a top-quartile capability-gap pitch. CTO reply: "We already have what you're describing." Slot wasted; credibility lost; LinkedIn callout risk. SR-02 probe blocks press-release inflation but not marketing-inflated JD language — the scorer reads JD text directly and is vulnerable to buzzword saturation in job descriptions.

False Negative — Quietly-Sophisticated-but-Silent

A B2B SaaS company with genuine production ML inference pipelines has a private GitHub, no engineering blog, and writes JDs without AI buzzwords ("builds inference services," not "builds AI/ML systems"). Scraper returns no signal; ai_maturity = 0. Pipeline routes to abstain or sends a generic email. This is the ideal Seg 4 client — private companies with mature but undisclosed ML practices are systematically underserved. Business impact: highest-value leads missed entirely.

§9 — Gap-Analysis Risks

Risk 1 — Deliberate capability gap: Some companies intentionally score below the sector AI benchmark because that domain is not their differentiation axis. A fintech payments processor that outsources all ML to a partner platform scores below peers on in-house ML tooling — but the gap is a deliberate vendor strategy, not an unmet need. The pipeline cannot detect intentional outsourcing; competitor_gap_brief does not distinguish gap-as-problem from gap-as-choice. SR-02 prevents score inflation from press releases but cannot detect deliberate outsourcing decisions.

Risk 2 — Sub-niche irrelevance: The competitor_gap_brief segments at the sector level ("fintech"). Embedding-based search is a top-quartile fintech practice — but irrelevant for a payments firm requiring real-time deterministic transaction logic, not semantic retrieval. The pipeline has no sub-niche awareness below the sector level; a capability gap real at sector level may be immaterial for the specific company's product.

§11 — One Honest Failure: SOC-01 (Regen-Failed Edge Case)

Probe: SOC-01 — assertive hiring/growth claims generated when weak_hiring_velocity_signal flag is set.
After Phase 5: SOC-01 moves FAIL → PASS via Python post-generation scan + up to 2 LLM regen attempts.
Unresolved: when both regen attempts still produce assertive language, the system appends assertive_claim_regen_failed and sends the original body — pipeline is NOT blocked. Est. trigger: <1% of leads ≈ 0.4 false-claim emails/week at 40 leads/wk. Low frequency, high severity — each is a verifiable false assertion to a real prospect: immediate hard-no, permanent credibility loss.
